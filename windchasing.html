"There is nothing new under the sun."

Using the Common English Bible (CEB) translation, local performer Benjamin Wesley brings the ancient words of the anonymous author often referred to as "Qoholet"--the Teacher--to vivid life in this one-hour presentation.

Ecclesiastes is both a work of "wisdom literature" (like the book of Proverbs that it comes after) and a subversion of it. "Remember," Qoholet tells us, "in much wisdom is much aggravation--the more knowledge, the more pain."

In his quest to find the meaning of life, Qoholet is both profound and surprisingly cynical, constantly repeating that everything is "chasing after wind," but affirming the goodness of resting in your work and enjoying the fruit of your labors--that is, if you're able to.

No matter whether you're Christian, atheist, something else, or something in-between, Wind-Chasing will be a thought-provoking and surprising production that will likely leave you saying, "Wait, that's in the Bible?"
